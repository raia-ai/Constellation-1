
# How to Resolve a "double" or "fuzzy" YouTube video Thumbnail image

How to Resolve a "double" or "fuzzy" YouTube video Thumbnail image

When viewing videos on YouTube from your YouTube Channel, have you noticed and having issues with the Thumbnail image looking fuzzy or it seems like a double image appears as if it is stuck between two photos?

A double image occurs when the thumbnail (randomly chosen frame) is showing a transition between two scenes.  For the videos that have this issue, this article will help you resolve the issue for the video that is having the double image problem.  For example:  see image below

![](https://constellation1.na3.teamsupport.com/api/attachments/image/2472652/562a93ed-4987-41ec-865e-1c403523c796.png)

**\*\* First!  We'll need to make some edits to your Paradym account in order to help potentially reduce this from happening in the future.    (Steps 1 - 4)**

**\*\* Second!  We'll need to make edits and corrections to the video from within your YouTube channel (Steps 5 - 9)**

Step 1: Log into your Paradym account, Then locate and select your name at the top right corner of your screen for a drop-down menu. Select the “Settings” option.

Step 2: Locate and select “Connections” from the menu of options on your left running vertically on the page.     Next, select the “Gear” icon to the right and beside YouTube. Image below

![](https://constellation1.na3.teamsupport.com/api/attachments/image/2472652/e527456b-ab17-48d0-ab35-ebcc94f3ff6d.png)

Step 3: Locate the small text that says:  " \* Requires your YouTube account to be verified. To verify your account, please visit **this link**. "  Go ahead and click on the link as shown below and follow the prompts to verify the account. See image below. It will walk you through the steps to verify your account by text or by email. Follow the prompts.

![](https://constellation1.na3.teamsupport.com/api/attachments/image/2472652/f7cbd00a-9b41-40f3-8b01-0ad3b22f69a2.png)

Step 4: Follow steps 1 and 2 in order to get you back to the screen you see in the image above in step 3.   Next, locate and "Select" the box next to the option that says:  "Use tour cover photo as YouTube Video thumbnail". Make sure the box has been checked.  Next, select "Save" in the top right part of your screen.   It should look like the image you see in step 3 above.

**Finished the First part!  Let's move on to the Second part!**

Step 5: Go to your YouTube channel on a new internet tab. The recommended web browser you should be using is Chrome when making these types of changes and edits. Log into your YouTube account and then locate your name (username, icon, initials, or photo) at the top right of your screen, select, for a drop down menu.   Select "Your Channel" from the menu of options as shown in the image below - Next, you'll want to Select "Videos" from the horizontal menu at the top of your screen. 

![](https://constellation1.na3.teamsupport.com/api/attachments/image/2472652/cae5a1d0-5f81-4f00-b27b-a028c4012988.png)

Step 6: Click on a “listing video” that had the double image you saw previously that had the double image problem. Click on the video of your choice. 

Step 7: Then, locate at the bottom, right below the video there are TWO small blue rectangle options that say Analytics and then one that says “Edit Video” , select the blue rectangle option that says “Edit Video.”

![](https://constellation1.na3.teamsupport.com/api/attachments/image/2472652/4312a7b4-be6c-4540-8c34-4edf3db881d7.png)

Step 8: Scroll down on the page some until you see options for “Thumbnail”.  It gives you three or more photo scenes to choose from. Select the one that makes the most sense. 

![](https://constellation1.na3.teamsupport.com/api/attachments/image/2472652/f7837b29-5de5-4eee-ba7e-a410fa170bde.png)

Step 9: Try clicking “Save” at the top right. If it doesn’t let you, scroll back down on the page and make your choice of audience, or any other options on the page it wants you to select and choose. After making your selections and changes, select “Save” at the top right in Blue.

Finished!
